Part 1: Multiple Choice

Choose the best answer for each question.

Q1

Texas is known as the "Lone Star State." What is the capital city of Texas?

Hint: This city is famous for music and has bats living under its Congress Avenue Bridge!

Q2

While New York City is the biggest city in New York, it is not the capital! Which city holds the title of New York's state capital?

Hint: This city sits along the Hudson River and is home to the Empire State Plaza.

Q3

California is the Golden State. What is California's state capital?

Hint: This city grew fast during the 1849 Gold Rush and is named after the river nearby.

Q4

Florida is known for its beautiful beaches and sunshine. What is the capital of Florida?

Hint: Its name comes from an indigenous Muskogean word meaning "old fields" or "abandoned town."

Q5

One US state capital is located exactly one mile high above sea level! Which of these capital cities sits in the Rocky Mountains?

Hint: This city is the capital of Colorado and is nicknamed the "Mile High City."

Q8

Part 4: Short Essays
